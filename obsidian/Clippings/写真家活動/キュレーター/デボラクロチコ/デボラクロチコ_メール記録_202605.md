### メール本文（英語）

Dear Deborah,

Thank you so much for your incredibly valuable response and for sharing the European Photography Curator Network document with me. I truly appreciate your continued support and guidance.

I apologize for not being able to respond immediately with a thorough review of the material. My graphic design work has been particularly demanding this week, and I haven't had enough time to properly read and digest all the information you've provided. Please allow me a bit more time to go through everything carefully. I have already started gathering information about Arles.

**Thames & Hudson Publishers**

Regarding Mr. Lee's reference to Thames & Hudson, he didn't specify whether it was the London or New York office. However, he showed me the website for "Flora Photographica" by William A. Ewing and Danaé Panchaud (https://www.thamesandhudson.com/products/flora-photographica-1?_pos=1&_sid=76b613373&_ss=r), and mentioned that they are planning a sequel and actively looking for photographers. He strongly encouraged me to reach out to them.

Are you familiar with William A. Ewing and Danaé Panchaud?

**Meeting with Ippodo Gallery**

I just returned from a meeting with Mr. Aono from Ippodo Gallery. He shared with me that selling photography is extremely challenging for him, and he has been struggling to sell Tonooka's work as well. However, I proposed to him that we work together toward museum acquisitions rather than focusing solely on sales.

As you well know—being the consummate professional that you are—I believe museum acquisitions serve as a powerful credential for artists, functioning almost like a certificate of legitimacy. With that foundation, I can then leverage that credibility to make more effective approaches, which will ultimately lead to sales opportunities.

Mr. Aono understood and embraced this philosophy. We concluded today's meeting with an agreement: in six months, I will print some photographs from my Snow series on washi paper and deliver them to him for this purpose.

During our conversation, Mr. Aono said something that deeply moved me: "You are stepping beyond the framework of being just a photographer and are trying to emerge as an artist. I support you wholeheartedly, though I regret that my own power is limited."

Of course, gallery support is important for selling work. But as you are currently demonstrating through your generous mentorship, I believe it is equally—if not more—important for me to take personal responsibility for building networks and ensuring that both my work and I as an artist are properly understood and recognized. Naturally, I must also continue creating work and maintaining the highest quality standards.

**Alt Photo Lab Project**

I apologize for not mentioning the lab grant earlier. I've been rushing through the grant application process to establish a darkroom lab dedicated to alternative photographic processes (with partial rental operations)—something that doesn't currently exist in Japan.

The lab has three core purposes:

1. **My own technical exploration**: To deepen my expertise in techniques I currently practice and to pursue interest in—and potentially acquire—techniques I have not yet explored.

2. **Preserving knowledge from master printers**: I have two teachers who taught me platinum/palladium printing. Both are active at the forefront of Japanese photography, but both are over 70 years old. I have spoken with them, and they are eager to pass their knowledge to the next generation.

3. **Educating the next generation and photography enthusiasts**: By promoting understanding of alternative processes, I hope to elevate the perceived value of handmade prints more broadly.

While I have no intention of working as a commercial printer, I hope to play a role in preserving and transmitting this technical knowledge. The project is still in its early stages, so we haven't published much information online yet, but I have launched **Ap2 Lab.** (https://altphotolab.com/).

I would love to share more details with you when we have a chance to speak.

Thank you again for all your support and invaluable guidance. I look forward to hearing from you about the European galleries and museums list.

Warm regards,
Daisuke Kinoshita
