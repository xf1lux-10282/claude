# Daisuke Kinoshita — Artist Statement

**Created**: February 28, 2026
**Version**: v9
**Purpose**: Artist Statement (Contemporary Art — Epistemological Approach)

---

We do not perceive the world.
We perceive the conditions through which the world becomes constituted.

An image requires visibility.
Distinction requires judgment.
Understanding requires meaning.

As long as these conditions function, the world appears stable and certain.
When they cease to function, the very formation of the image falters.

My practice does not address existence itself.
It addresses the conditions through which existence becomes certain.

---

Information proliferates beyond measure. Judgment accelerates. Seasons fracture.
The conditions through which we understand the world are quietly beginning to unravel.

In this era, photography is not a means of recording something.
It is a device that exposes the conditions of perception.

Objects are not recorded.
The conditions through which objects are seen are dismantled.

---

I use platinum/palladium printing and gelatin silver printing.
Not for archival permanence, but to materialize the conditions of perception.

Platinum absorbs light. The image recedes into the interior of the paper.
Gelatin silver reflects light. The image shifts as the viewer moves.

The choice of materials is not symbolic. It is an act of constructing the conditions of perception themselves.

---

This approach resonates with a tradition—
concealment as revelation (Zeami, Tanizaki).

Yet this is not an illustration of aesthetics.
From the same epistemological stance, I reexamine contemporary conditions.

---

The work does not stabilize the viewer's perception.
It constructs the experience of conditions ceasing to function—as material.

When seeing does not converge into understanding,
the conditions that had constituted perception are revealed for the first time.

---
