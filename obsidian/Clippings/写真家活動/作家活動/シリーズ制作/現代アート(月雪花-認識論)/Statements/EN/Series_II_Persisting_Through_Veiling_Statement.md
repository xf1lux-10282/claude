# II. Persisting Through Veiling
## Series Statement

**Daisuke Kinoshita**
**February 26, 2026**

---

## Condition of Judgment

In snow, boundaries become ambiguous.
Yet existence persists.

Judgment depends on boundaries.
Snow veils these boundaries, destabilizing distinction.

Boundaries do not disappear, but cease to function.
The perceptual work of separating and confirming is held in suspension.

Distinction becomes possible, yet confirmation does not follow.
Perception remains suspended before conclusion.

Ambiguity of boundaries destabilizes judgment.
Judgment is not given—it depends on conditions.

Certainty dissolves.
Existence remains.

---

## Technical Information

**Medium**: Gelatin silver prints
**Sizes**: 8×10 inches to 16×20 inches
**Number of works**: 10-15
**Production period**: 2024–ongoing

**Material rationale**:
Silver gelatin reflects light. The image shifts as the viewer moves.
Judgment cannot stabilize.
