# Ruten
## Sacred Flux

**Daisuke Kinoshita**
**February 28, 2026**

---

## This Is Not a Story of Water

Water is
not a subject of documentation,
nor a symbol.

It is

**a device that exposes how meaning is constructed through relationships.**

---

## The Core Question

**How is meaning constructed through relationships?**

The same water
appears as different entities
through different relationships.

Water that is prayed to is sacred.
Water that flows carries time.
Water that is consumed is a resource.
Water that is brewed mediates culture.

**Water itself (H₂O) does not change.**
**But when the relationship changes, water becomes a different entity.**

Meaning does not inhere in objects.
It emerges only through relationships.

---

## Goko Shrine as a Case Study

This project is photographed
primarily at Goko Shrine in Fushimi, Kyoto.

But this is not a documentation of a specific place.

Goko Shrine functions as
**a site where multiple relationships coexist simultaneously.**

### The Same Water Source Appearing as Multiple Entities

- **As an object of prayer**: Water for rituals praying for abundant harvests
- **As a medium of time**: Groundwater circulating through the earth
- **As a medium of culture**: Fine water used in sake brewing
- **As a tourist resource**: "Fushimi's famous water" as a commodity

These are the same water source.
Yet through different relationships, they hold different meanings.

---

## What Is Ruten?

"Ruten" refers to eternal cycles in Buddhist thought.

However, what this project questions is
not **material circulation**
but **the fluidity of meaning.**

Water circulates.
In the process, water dons and sheds multiple meanings.

From sacred to resource.
From resource to culture.
From culture to consumption.

**Meaning is not fixed.**
**When relationships change, meaning changes.**

---

## A Shift in the Question

The current statement asks:

> Are we now merely consuming as a resource what we once held sacred through prayer?

But the essential question is this:

**Why does the same object appear as different meanings?**

This is not a moral question.
It is an epistemological question.

Where does meaning reside?
Within the object, or within the relationship?

---

## Platinum Printing as Contradiction

This project employs
platinum printing techniques from the 19th century.

Platinum fixes directly to paper fibers.
The image is fixed.
It does not change.

**But what is photographed is flowing water.**

A fixed image
captures a fluid existence.

This contradiction is not a technical limitation.
It materializes the tension between **the desire to fix meaning** and **the fluidity of existence.**

We want to fix what flows.
Make permanent what changes.
Reduce multiple meanings to a single truth.

The permanence of platinum printing
exposes that very desire.

---

## Project Positioning

"Ruten" is

an epistemological project that exposes

**not that meaning inheres in objects,**
**but the process through which meaning is constructed through relationships.**

This extends
"Conditions of Perception" (the Moon-Snow-Flower trilogy).

```
Conditions of Perception: How does existence appear when frameworks of meaning cease to function?
Conditions of Relationship: How does existence appear when multiple frameworks of meaning coexist?
```

If Moon-Snow-Flower questions "the dissolution of meaning,"
Ruten questions "the multiplicity of meaning."

---

## What the Work Does

This project does not document water.

It asks the viewer:

**Through which relationship are you seeing this water?**

As an object of prayer?
As circulating matter?
As a resource to be consumed?
As something mediating culture?

The answer depends on the relationship in which the viewer stands.

**Meaning emerges only through the relationship between viewer and object.**

- The image sinks into the paper

- The fixed image capturing fluid existence materializes the contradiction
